Our Story

Omar & Nour Photo Placeholder

Every love story is beautiful, but ours is our favorite. From the moment we met, we knew this was the beginning of something extraordinary. Omar fixed shoes in a small shop near the Nile. Nour sold bread in the market across the street. Every morning, he saved her a fresh loaf. Every morning, she left him a glass of sweet tea. One day, Omar said, "I have nothing but calloused hands and a leaking roof." Nour laughed. "And I have flour under my nails and a broom for a husband." "Then marry me," he said. "Only if you teach me to dance first," she replied. So that evening, by the river, Omar hummed an old tune and held Nour like she was made of feathers. She stepped on his feet three times. He didn't mind. Her father said no. Omar was too poor. Nour packed one dress, two coins, and a loaf of bread. "Where are you going?" her father asked. "To Omar," she said. "And then to anywhere else." They walked two days to a small village. Omar found work building houses. Nour sold bread from a cart. At night, they shared one blanket and told each other what they would buy one day—a door that locks, a second blanket, a chicken. They never got the chicken. But forty years later, their grandchildren found a box under their bed. Inside: a dried loaf of bread and a note. This is from the first morning she loved me. I never threw it away.

Our Forever Oath

"Today, surrounded by those we love, we promise to choose each other every day. Through every sunrise and every season, we vow to walk side by side, to support one another's dreams, to find joy in the smallest moments, and to build a lifetime of beautiful memories together. Wherever life leads us, our hearts will always find their way home to each other. This is our promise, our journey, and our forever."
